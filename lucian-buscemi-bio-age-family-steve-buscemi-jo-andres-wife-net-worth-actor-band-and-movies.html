Lucian Buscemi Biography

Lucian Buscemi is an American Actor, Musician, Director and Producer who was born in New York and brought-up in Park Slope, Brooklyn. He is widely known as the son of the renowned American Actor, Director, Writer and Producer, Steve Buscemi. He formed a band titled ‘Fiasco’, which he collaborated with two of his friends, Jonathan Edelsten, a guitarist and vocalist and Julian Bennett Holmes, a drummer.

The Fiasco band focuses in heavy rock punk, noise rock, and math rock, they released their debut album ‘God Loves Fiasco’ on 24 June 2007. The band recorded other albums, which include, Native Canadians, Fabulous Bozo FP, and Sinus Rhythm Breakdown. He has also appeared on notable films and series including, ‘Sal’, ‘Interview’, ‘Tree Lounges’, ‘The Sopranos’ and ‘4:44 Last Day on Earth’.

    Lucian Buscemi Family, Parents and Siblings

    Lucian was born to Jo Andres and Steve Buscemi in New York City. Steven is an American actor, director, writer and producer, known for a number of successful movies, including Parting Glances, New York Stories. His grandparents are Dorothy Buscemi and John Buscemi. His mother Andres died on January 6, 2019, at the age of 64, after suffering from encapsulating peritoneal sclerosis, a rare disease of the bowel. She was born in Wichita, Kansas in 1954.

    Andres first became known on the kinetic downtown New York performance scene of the 1980s for her film/dance/light performances, shown at the Performing Garage, La Mama Experimental Theater Club P.S. 122, St. Marks Danspace, and the Collective for Living Cinema. As a filmmaker, Andres won Awards for the 1996 film Black Kites which aired on PBS and played several film festivals, including Sundance, Berlin, Toronto, London, and Human Rights Watch Film Festivals.
