Who Is Chris Rock's New Girlfriend Lake Bell?

Publish date: 2024-08-25

When it comes to the lives of the rich and famous, people can't help but be interested in their love lives. Celebrity couples have been a mainstay in the headlines for ages, as people love knowing who's dating who these days. Whether it's a quick divorce, couples who got together on reality TV, or even those that are making it work, people enjoy talking about celebrity love lives.

Chris Rock recently made waves when he made things public with Lake Bell. The actress has been involved in some high-profile projects, and we have everything you need to know about Rock's new love interest below.

Chris Rock Recently Began Dating Lake Bell

By this point, the vast majority of people are familiar with Chris Rock and his comedic stylings. He has been a popular name in the entertainment industry for a couple decades now, and Rock has an unquestioned place in history thanks to the body of work that he has put together.

As if being a dynamic stand-up comedian wasn't solid enough for the performer, Chris Rock eventually set his sites to the big screen, and he landed roles in a number of successful movies. This was instrumental in him becoming a household name.

Rock has been featured in the Madagascar franchise, the Grown Ups movies, The Longest Yard, Lethal Weapon 4, New Jack City, Dr. Dolittle, and a whole lot more.

On the small screen, Rock got a major break in 1990 when he first began his time on Saturday Night Live. He even did a brief stent on In Living Color following his time on SNL. In the years since then, he has had a number of successful shows, including The Chris Rock Show, and Everybody Hates Chris.

Rock normally makes headlines for his work on the big screen, but his personal life has gotten some attention, too. In fact, he recently began dating Lake Bell, a performer that should look familiar to many.

She's Done Movies Like 'Spider-Man: Into The Spider-Verse'

Having been in the entertainment industry since the 2000s, Lake Bell as a performer that many people know. While not a major name, she's been everywhere through the years, and her accomplishments are quite impressive.

On the big screen, she got her start in 2002's Speakeasy, and from there, she would steadily chip away while earning larger roles in more prominent films.

Movies like What Happens in Vegas, Pride and Glory, and It's Complicated helped her round out the 2000s, and in the 2010s, she continued landing roles in various films.

Bell has done some outstanding voice acting work, and in 2018, she landed the role of Vanessa Fisk in Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse.

Looking head, rumors have surfaced that the actress will be appearing in Black Panther: Wakanda Forever.

"In a recent series of photos from the set of Black Panther: Wakanda Forever posted to Instagram, Atlanta Filming teased that there were three new cast members showcased in the pictures. The first photo in the series of three is a group photo of both cast and crew. While many of the faces are wearing masks or turned slightly away from the camera, one face is standing front and center. And it seems to be the face of actress and voice talent Lake Bell," Cosmic Circus writes.

Clearly, Bell has done well in film, but she's also found a way to thrive on the small screen, too.

She's Done Shows Like 'Harley Quinn'

On television, Lake Bell has been a mainstay for years.

Early on, she landed a brief role on ER, and she appeared on several episodes of The Practice. This was followed by a 14-episode run on Boston Legal, which was a huge hit during the 2000s. Boston Legal really helped get everything going for the actress.

She would subsequently land roles on shows like Surface, Children's Hospital, The League, How to Make It in America, and more.

Much like her time in film, Bell has done a lot of voice acting work on the small screen. She has lent her voice to projects like a BoJack Horseman, SuperMansion, Marvel's What If...?,and even Harley Quinn, where she voices Poison Ivy and Barbara Gordon.

Bell even created and produced Bless This Mess, which co-starred Dax Shepard and ran for two seasons before meeting an end in May 2020.
