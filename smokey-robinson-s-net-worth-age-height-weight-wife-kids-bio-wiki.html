Early Life & Biography

Smokey Robinson was born on 19 February in Detroit, Michigan, US. He was born to William Robinson Sr. and Flossie. His parents have mixed French and Black ancestry. During his childhood days, his family was not wealthy, but his parents always supported the pursuits of their son. Robinson was not the above-average student but also was a good athlete.

Robinson attended Northern High School, where he started to demonstrate his musical talent. After that, he enrolled at the college to pursue his studies in electrical engineering. But later, he dropped out the college after two months so that he can pursue his career in music.

Along with his classmate Pete Moore and childhood friend Ronald White, he formed a doo-wop group named the Five Chimes. Later, they changed their group’s name to The Matadors in the year 1957.

Later in the same year, Bobby Rogers join their group. After that, Bobby’s cousin, Claudette, was added to the group as well as guitarist Marv Tarplin was also added in the group in 1958. The group started performing t various venues in Detroit that increase their reputation.

Career

During school time, Robinson formed a doo-wop group that was named the Five Chimes. Smokey Robinson was the frontman and founder of the group The Miracles and also known as the first artist who signed to the legendary Motown Records.

Robinson was the chief songwriter as well as producer of the group until 1972 when he got retired to become the Motown’s Vice president. Robinson had 26 top forty hits as the part of The Miracles in between the years 1960 and 1970. The hit songs in the group include I Second That Emotion, You’ve Got a Hold on Me, Baby Baby Don’t Cry, and Mickey’s Monkey. Apart from this, ‘Tears of a Clown’ was the one hit song of the group during that time. His career has spanned over 50 years, and he made lots of achievements in the music field.

Awards & Achievements

In February 1983, Robinson was awarded The Hollywood Walk of Fame. Later, in 1987, he was inducted to the Rock & Roll Hall of Fame. He was also awarded the Grammy Award in 1988. He was also awarded a medal at the National Medal of Arts, and Soul Train Music Awards. He was also awarded the Society of Singers Lifetime Achievement Award in the year 2011. In the year 2015, he also received a BET Lifetime Achievement Award.
