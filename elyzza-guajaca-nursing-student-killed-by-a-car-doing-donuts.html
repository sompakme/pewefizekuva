Elyzza Guajaca, Nursing Student, Killed By A Car Doing Donuts

Publish date: 2024-09-25

Elyzza Guajaca was a 24-year-old who was killed by a car. The local authorities reported that the incident happened on Sunday around 9 PM. 

The authorities also said they are searching to find the person who was convicted for a hit-and-run case.

The person was alleged of the death of a woman at a street takeover in the Hyde Park community of South Los Angeles on Christmas night.

The local authorities said the incident happened near the intersection of Crenshaw Boulevard and West Florence Avenue.  

When the police were called to the incident spot, an investigation was opened as it looked to be a hit-and-run case.

The victim was identified to be 24-year-old Elyzza Guajaca by her family. 

The police said the woman was struck and killed by a car that was seen doing donuts in the middle of the intersection.

She was immediately escorted to the nearby hospital, where the doctors declared her dead from her injuries. 

Louie Guajaca, her brother, wrote on a GoFundMe campaign, “My sister was an aunty to my beautiful girls who loved her so much. She was a beautiful sister who loved big. She was a daughter who always wanted to be with others and have family time. She was the rock to our family.“

A member of Elyzza Guajaca’s family told a strong source that before her death, she worked in a nursing school.

She also worked at the Los Angeles International Airport and later built a strong interest in car culture after she watched the first “Fast and Furious” movie. 

She loves the kinds of events similar to what she visited on Sunday and also on the day she was killed.

According to the police, the driver hit Elyzza and then left the vehicle there, and ran away.
