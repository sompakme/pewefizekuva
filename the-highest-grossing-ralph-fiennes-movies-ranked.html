The Highest-grossing Ralph Fiennes Movies, Ranked

Ralph Fiennes is an extraordinary English actor, producer, and director. After graduating from the Royal Academy of Dramatic Arts, Ralph delivered fantastic on-screen and on-stage performances. Fiennes rose to stardom after his noticeable role as a Nazi Commandant in the 1993 Schindler’s List, earning him an Academy Award nomination. With a stellar 20-year career on the silver screen, Ralph has acquired a net worth of $60 million, with 23 leading roles and 15 supporting roles under his belt. Besides acting, he is also an accredited director, renowned for his creative work in films like King’s Man and Coriolanus.

Apart from acting, Ralph has worked as a voice artist and has given many famous characters their trademark voice, like Alfred from The LEGO Batman Movie. Here is a list of 8 Ralph Fiennes’s highest-grossing movies, ranked by box office.

Quiz Show (1994) - $24.8 Million

Robert Redford’s exploration of a rigged game show and the leading investigation is portrayed beautifully in Quiz Show. The film’s dramatic storyline earned a worldwide box office collection of $25 Million, as per Box Office Mojo. Ralph Fiennes, John Turturro, and Rob Morrow’s terrific performance has earned many accolades and nominations, including the Oscars.

Ralph Fiennes is a brilliant actor, accredited director, and an English gentleman. With many films under his belt, his versatile acting career is a testament to the actor’s ability to understand the essence of the character and deliver record-breaking box office performances.

The Menu (2022) - $38.5 Million

Ralph Fiennes’s latest appearance as Chef Slowik in the 2022 dark comedy thriller, The Menu portrays the actor in a different role. Rather than playing a more prominent villain, Chef Slowik’s creepy claps and eerily calm use of voice have been a film highlight. The $3 million movie grossed $38 million domestically and $79 million internationally. This culinary comedy was another addition to Ralph’s versatile acting list.

The Grand Budapest Hotel (2014) - $59.1 Million

Wes Anderson’s adventurous comedy, The Grand Budapest Hotel, was highly acclaimed by critics and nominated for the Oscars. This was one of the best performances in Fiennes’s esteemed career. The film collected $59 Million domestically and $173 Million worldwide. Fiennes delivered a layered performance as M. Gustav, filled with emotions and charm. Fiennes, F. Murray Abraham, and Saoirse Ronan were able to bag nine Academy Award nominations for the film with their magnificent performance.

The English Patient (1996) - $78.7 Million

Propelling the actor towards more leading roles, The English Patient shows Ralph Fiennes in a more sensitive stance where he brings his Shakespearean tragedy to the character. Working with Juliette Binoche, the two actors have brought war tragedies and brutalities to the audience in a classic novel-like fashion. With a production budget of just $27 Million, The English Patient collected $232 Million worldwide.

Schindler’s List (1993) - $96.6 Million

Directed by the genius Steven Spielberg, Schindler’s List is a cinematic masterpiece and one of the most influential movies of the 20th century. Based on the World War II setting, Schindler’s List talks about an industrialist saving the Polish-Jewish refugees. According to Gold Derby, Ralph got close to the character and captured his essence. The Holocaust Drama won Academy Awards for Best Picture, Best Cinematography, and other categories, while Fiennes was nominated for Best Supporting Actor. The wartime epic made a worldwide box office of $321 Million and was one of the highest-ranking films of 1993.

The LEGO Batman Movie (2017) - $175.7 Million

The LEGO Batman Movie, as the name suggests, centers around the Dark Knight and his enemy, the Joker, only in Legoland. In this 2017 kids’ film, Ralph Fiennes voiced the famous character of Alfred Pennyworth, Batman’s loyal butler. The film’s star-studded cast, including celebrities like Will Arnett, Zach Galifianakis, and Michael Cera, received positive reviews for the cinematic animation. Legoland was also 2010’s much-loved film, according to Collider. The Lego-oriented movie was a huge hit worldwide and earned a box office collection of $312 Million.

Skyfall (2012) - $304.4 Million

Making yet another marvelous appearance in the action thriller Skyfall, Fiennes plays the role of the antagonist, Gareth Mallory. Working alongside Fiennes are A-lister's like Daniel Craig and Naomie Harris. With the gripping storyline and the stellar star cast, the film grossed over $304 million in the United States. With a worldwide box office collection of $1.109 Billion, Skyfall is one of the highest-ranking films in Ralph’s stellar acting career.

The Harry Potter Series (2005 - 2011) - $7.75 Billion

One of the highest-earning movies of all time, the Harry Potter series is based on the billionaire writer J.K. Rowling's novel, Harry Potter, boasts a stellar cast including Daniel Radcliff, Emma Watson, Rupert Grint, Alan Rickman, and Ralph Fiennes. Ralph played the iconic role of ‘Lord Voldemort’ and excelled at it. A considerable chunk of his $60 Million net worth comes from the Harry Potter franchise. The actor was paid about $50 million collectively, as per TheThings.
