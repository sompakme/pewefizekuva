Jake Owen’s Early Life and Career Journey

Jake Owen, born on August 28, 1981, in Winter Haven, Florida, had dreams of becoming a professional golfer as a child. However, a wakeboarding accident altered the course of his life and led him down a different path – the world of music.

After the accident, Jake Owen discovered a passion for music and decided to pursue it wholeheartedly. He taught himself how to play the guitar and began performing at local venues, building a loyal fan base in his hometown.

With a growing ambition to make it big in the music industry, Owen made the bold move to Nashville, Tennessee. It was here that his talent caught the attention of Sony BMG Nashville executives, eventually leading to a record deal with RCA Nashville in 2005.

One year later, in 2006, Jake Owen released his debut single “Yee Haw,” which quickly climbed the charts and marked the beginning of his successful career. His unique country sound resonated with audiences, earning him a dedicated following.

Throughout his career, Jake Owen has released several albums, each showcasing his growth as an artist. From his debut album “Startin’ with Me” to his chart-topping albums “American Love” and “Greetings from…Jake,” his discography is a testament to his undeniable talent.
