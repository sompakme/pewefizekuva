 Eddy Grant Biography

Eddy Grant is a Guyanese British singer-songwriter and musician and was a founding member of “The Equals”, one of the United Kingdom’s first racially integrated pop groups. He was born and bought up in Plaisance, British Guiana as Edmond Montague Grant. His subsequent solo career included the platinum single “Electric Avenue”. He also pioneered the genre ringbang.

    Eddy Grant Education

    Grant went to school at the Acland Burghley Secondary Modern at Tufnell Park, where he learned to read and write music. He became a big fan of Chuck Berry, and after seeing him play at the Finsbury Park Astoria decided on a career in music.

    Eddy Grant Parents

    His father, Patrick Alexander Grant, was a trumpeter who played in Nello and the Luckies. While at school, his parents lived and worked in the United Kingdom, sending back money for his education. In 1960, he emigrated to join his parents in London. His brother is Rudy Grant.
